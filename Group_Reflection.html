Individual Reflection

Abhinav Manohar

Working as part of the team was a lot of fun, and I learned a lot in the process. As a group, we assigned roles and responsibilities to each member, allowing everyone to contribute equally. Working in groups has increased my collaborative abilities. Working in a group provided me with new views and enabled me to address more complicated issues than I could alone. We held frequent meetings (2 official meetings with all members and 3-4 informal meetings while working on the project) and used to create meeting agendas and minutes, much like the prior time, which helped me learn how to plan ahead and manage my time properly.

Our group had six members, all of whom I found to be extremely supportive, which surprised me. My teammates had to acquire new skills along the way so that they could work towards the project, such as Figma, Blender and Moviestorm softwares. It was difficult when they started off working with it but after a few days they became comfortable with it and produced the results as expected. I was given the task of designing the architectural prototype model for Food Tunnelz, and I was in charge of two sections: Aims and Plans and Progress. We utilised Microsoft Teams and Canvas for meetings, announcements, and discussions, Google Docs for sharing our work, and github for sharing the group website.

Group Reflection

What went well?

As in assignment 2, we were able to manage and organise our group in such a way that everyone had an equal amount of workload to complete, and we prioritised our work accordingly so that everyone in the group could complete their share of work before each deadline. We made sure that certain aspects of assignments were completed before certain dates so that we had ample time to fix any errors or issues before the due time.

We began the assignment by identifying and discussing the errors and mistakes the team made in the previous assignment. We discussed on how we can make sure theses mistakes and errors do not repeat. The team as a whole did a good job in stopping a majority of these from occurring again.

What could have been improved?

There were again times where we had difficulty in conducting our meetings at the pre-planned time as some members were occupied with their work or were not at a place where they could appropriately join the meeting. Therefore, meetings had to be cancelled or postponed. This had a negative impact on the assignment and the timely preparation of it. Improving on that would mean that any work which has not been completed by any of the team members can be completed timely, and so we do not have to rush in any way before the deadline and submit our work in an excellent quality.

What is one thing that you have learned about groups?

One thing we need to stress on is that every member of the group is dedicated, smart and has their own strengths. Each member of the group contributed the required amount of work and in fact more than what was required to complete the assignment. This was despite of their busy work life or the unbelievable pandemic situation in India where two of our members are based in. It was again clearly evident that working in groups remotely is not as easy as you would think, as the situations that each of our members are in varied a lot.

Our GitHub logs does not represent how well our group performed on the assignment as we divided work equally with everyone. Our group decided that only one person will work on website and each of the other individuals will work on their part of assignment. This was done as that one person was highly knowledgeable in HTML site development. Instead of GitHub the minutes of the formal group meetings, messages and even informal group meeting on teams clearly represent how well the group worked on the assignment.
